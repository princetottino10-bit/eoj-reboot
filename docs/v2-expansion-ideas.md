# EoJ Reboot v2 Expansion Ideas

## Goal

These ideas are for future polish, expansion packs, seasonal variants, or late-stage feature growth.
They are intentionally broader than the immediate v2 simulation spec.

## Design Principle

The game becomes richer when it adds:

- new decisions
- new deck identities
- new board stories

without adding too many unreadable states.

So the best expansion content is usually:

- new board rules
- new resource pressures
- new deckbuilding hooks
- new ace expressions

rather than endless new hard-status effects.

## Expansion Track 1: Board Variety

### 1. Map Presets

Different 3x3 board templates with fixed elemental layouts.

Examples:
- Symmetric classic map
- Center-dominant map
- Corner-heavy map
- Dual-lane map

Why it is good:
- Adds meta variety without rewriting card text
- Lets factions value different openings
- Easy seasonal content

### 2. Terrain Cells

Add a few named tile types on top of base element.

Examples:
- Bastion: first physical hit each turn is reduced
- Relay: summon here and gain +1 mana next turn
- Sniper Nest: ranged attacks from here gain +1 ATK
- Rift: a rotated unit on this tile takes +1 damage

Why it is good:
- Gives map identity
- Supports faction flavor without new status clutter

### 3. Rotating Featured Boards

Weekly or monthly "featured arena" rules.

Examples:
- Trick arena: extra reward for blind-spot setups
- Tank arena: center cells start fortified
- Snipe arena: corner cells gain range bonuses

Why it is good:
- Great for live-service style rotation
- Reuses the same card pool

## Expansion Track 2: Deckbuilding Luxury

### 4. Signature Slot

Each deck gets 1 signature card slot.
That slot must be filled by a card from one chosen faction.

Why it is good:
- Makes dual-faction decks feel less samey
- Easy identity hook for expansions

### 5. Doctrine Cards

Instead of only item set A/B/C/D, add one passive doctrine per deck.

Examples:
- Blitz Doctrine: first attack each turn gains +1 ATK
- Fortress Doctrine: first covered ally each turn heals 1
- Opportunist Doctrine: first blind-spot hit each turn gains +1 VP if it kills

Why it is good:
- Expands strategy without exploding the card list
- Useful for future sets and events

### 6. Ace Package Option

Let a player choose one of two ace package variants for a faction.

Examples:
- Trick ace: board-break version or pickoff version
- Tank ace: fortress version or counterpunch version
- Control ace: resource drain version or debuff swarm version

Why it is good:
- Same faction can support different archetypes
- Excellent expansion material

## Expansion Track 3: Match Flow Upgrades

### 7. Public Objectives

Add one or two visible match objectives worth VP.

Examples:
- First to attack with 3 different allies: +2 VP
- End turn controlling center and one corner: +2 VP
- Kill a C5+ enemy: +2 VP

Why it is good:
- Makes turns more varied
- Encourages route switching
- Supports comeback lines

### 8. Escalation Thresholds

At turn 8 or turn 10, a global rule changes.

Examples:
- Second summon surcharge drops from +1 to +0
- First ace summoned each side costs 1 less
- Kill VP for C5-C6 increases by 1

Why it is good:
- Smooths the transition into late-game
- Makes ace plans more reliable

### 9. Overtime Rules

If neither route wins by turn 50, enter overtime instead of flat ending.

Examples:
- Every turn both players gain +1 VP
- Board control grants +1 VP at end of turn
- First ace kill in overtime wins instantly

Why it is good:
- Dramatic endings
- Better spectator experience

## Expansion Track 4: Faction Luxury

### 10. Faction-Specific Mini Resources

Keep them light and visible.

Examples:
- Trick: misdirection counters from successful blind-spot setups
- Tank: guard counters from successful cover events
- Control: pressure counters from applied debuffs
- Synergy: resonance counters from marked allies
- Snipe: sight counters from safe attacks
- Aggro: fury counters from successful attacks this turn

Why it is good:
- Great for high-end ace cards
- Gives factions stronger identity

Warning:
- Only do this if the base game is already readable

### 11. Elite Supporters

Small set of neutral C3-C4 cards that reward one route or faction style.

Examples:
- VP enabler
- board-anchor utility
- anti-control cleanser
- anti-blind-spot defender

Why it is good:
- Lets players patch faction weaknesses
- Good expansion material without full faction overhauls

### 12. Legendary Aces

Alternate ace versions that are clearly splashy and rarer.

Examples:
- "final form" ace variants
- route-specific alternate finishers
- expansion-only boss-tier finishers

Why it is good:
- High excitement
- Easy headline content for a set

## Expansion Track 5: Presentation and Feel

### 13. Named Combo Tags

Do not add rules burden. Just surface cool patterns.

Examples:
- Blind-spot chain
- Fortress line
- Snipe corridor
- Resonance field

Why it is good:
- Makes the game feel richer
- Helps players notice emergent play

### 14. Faction Showcase Scenarios

Single-player or puzzle-style setups.

Examples:
- Win via VP with Control in 3 turns
- Force 5-cell victory with Tank
- Create 3 blind-spot attackers as Trick

Why it is good:
- Teaches identities
- Excellent for onboarding and premium content

### 15. Seasonal Modifier Packs

Light rulesets that rotate in and out.

Examples:
- All aces cost 1 less
- Kill VP doubled for C5-C6
- Element bonuses are stronger
- Second summon is free every third turn

Why it is good:
- Fast content cadence
- Encourages experimentation

## Best Short-Term "Luxury" Additions

These are the most practical without overcomplicating the current game:

1. Map presets
2. Public objectives
3. Doctrine cards
4. Ace package variants
5. Faction showcase scenarios

## Best Long-Term Expansion Hooks

These are larger but potentially excellent later:

1. Terrain cells
2. Faction-specific mini resources
3. Legendary ace variants
4. Overtime rules
5. Seasonal modifier packs

## What To Avoid

- Too many new hard crowd-control states
- Too many invisible counters
- Expansion ideas that ignore facing and position entirely
- Luxury systems that make the AI dramatically worse
- New mechanics that only matter on one or two cards

## Recommended Philosophy

Use expansions to make the game feel broader, not noisier.

The strongest expansion content will:

- deepen route choice
- make aces more expressive
- make boards more memorable
- add deck identity

without making players track ten extra statuses every turn.
